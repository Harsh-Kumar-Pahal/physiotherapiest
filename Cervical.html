Cervical Spondylosis Treatment

Effective care for cervical spine health, addressing pain, stiffness, and improved mobility

Cervical Spondylosis

Cervical spondylosis is a common, age-related condition that affects the joints and discs in your cervical spine, which is in your neck. Symptoms can include pain, stiffness, and limited range of motion. Treatment typically involves physical therapy, medications, and specific exercises to manage symptoms and improve flexibility.

Frozen Shoulder

Frozen shoulder, also known as adhesive capsulitis, causes pain and stiffness in the shoulder joint. It usually develops over time and can last for several years if not treated. Effective treatments include physical therapy, stretching, and, in some cases, injections to reduce inflammation.

Arthritis Pain

Arthritis pain affects many individuals and can result from joint inflammation, stiffness, and discomfort. Management often involves a combination of physical therapy, medications, and lifestyle adjustments to reduce pain and improve joint mobility, helping to maintain an active life.

Dry Needling

Dry needling is a technique used to relieve muscle pain and stiffness. This treatment targets trigger points in the muscles to release tension and promote healing. It is particularly useful for patients with chronic pain conditions and can be an effective component of a larger treatment plan for cervical spondylosis.
